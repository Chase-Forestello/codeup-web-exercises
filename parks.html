National Parks FAQ

How many people visit the national parks?(click)
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the National Park System?(click)
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?(click)
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?(click)
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.
How many areas are in the National Park System?(click)
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?(click)
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres.
What is the National Park Service budget?(click)
Fiscal Year (FY) 2014 Enacted: $2.98 billion.

How do I apply for a job with the National Park Service?(click)
National Park Service jobs, including both permanent and seasonal positions, are listed on USAJOBs.
What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?(click)
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.

Canyonlands(click)

  • Canyonlands was made a national park on September 12, 1964
  • Butch Cassidy's gang hideout was located here.
  • Canyonlands has a combined area equivalent to 172,121 football fields.
  • The highest elevation found in Canyonlands is 7,120 feet at Cathedral Point

Yosemite(click)

  • Yosemite was made a national park on October 1, 1890.
  • Yosemite contains giant Sequoias approximately 3,000 years old.
  • John Muir, the father of conservation, spearheaded Yosemite's creation.
  • Yosemite valley was formed by giant glaciers over one million years ago.

Yellowstone(click)

  • Yellowstone was made a national park on March 1, 1872
  • There are more than 10,000 hydrothermal features in Yellowstone.
  • There are more than 500 active geysers.
  • Yellowstone is home to the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48.
